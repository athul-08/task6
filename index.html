Cricket

Cricket is a bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of eleven players each on a circular field. Originating in England, it has become one of the world's most popular sports, particularly in countries like India, Australia, England, and South Africa. The game is played with a bat and ball, where one team bats while the other bowls and fields. The batting team scores runs by hitting the ball and running between two sets of wickets, while the bowling team tries to dismiss the batsmen through various means, such as catching the ball, hitting the wickets, or trapping the batsman in front of the wicket. Cricket matches can last for a few hours to several days, with different formats like Test cricket, One Day Internationals (ODIs), and Twenty20 (T20) cricket offering varying durations and styles of play.

Football

Football is a popular sport played between two teams of eleven players each on a rectangular field. It is widely regarded as the world's most popular sport, with millions of fans and players globally. The objective of the game is to score goals by getting the ball into the opposing team's goal, using any part of the body except the hands and arms (except for the goalkeepers). Football matches are played over two halves, each typically lasting 45 minutes, with additional time added for stoppages. The team with the most goals at the end of the match wins.

Volleyball

Volleyball is a team sport played with a ball and a net. The objective is to send the ball over the net and into the opponent's court in a way that the opponent cannot return it. Each team has three hits to return the ball to the opponent's court. The game is played in sets, with each set typically played to 25 points (must win by 2 points). Volleyball is popular worldwide, with many variations of the game played in different countries.

Hockey

Hockey is a fast-paced sport played on a field or on ice, in which two teams use sticks to hit a ball into the opponent's goal. It is played in many variations, including field hockey and ice hockey, and is popular in many countries around the world. Hockey matches are typically played in two halves, each lasting 35-45 minutes, depending on the level of play. The team with the most goals at the end of the match wins.
